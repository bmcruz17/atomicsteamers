Employee Training

Optima Steamer XD operation, PPE, and bed bug treatment procedures.

⚠️ Training template — verify before use. These procedures are a starting point based on standard diesel vapor-steamer and bed bug remediation practice. Confirm every step against the official Optima Steamer XD operator's manual, the equipment's safety labels, and OSHA requirements, and edit this course to match Atomic Steamers' own SOPs. When the manual and this course disagree, the manual wins.
1 PPE & Personal Decontamination — Don't take bed bugs home

Wear on every bed bug job:

  • Disposable coveralls (Tyvek-style), smooth and tucked — bed bugs can't grip or hide in them.
  • Nitrile gloves; heat-resistant gloves when handling the steam wand or hot surfaces.
  • Closed-toe non-porous boots or disposable shoe covers.
  • N95 respirator and safety glasses (steam, dust, allergens).
Bed bugs hitchhike on clothing, bags, and shoes. Treat every job as if the bugs are trying to follow you home.

End-of-job decon (before you get in the vehicle):

  1. Peel coveralls off inside-out and seal them in a plastic bag.
  2. Bag gloves/shoe covers. Wipe boots; inspect soles and seams.
  3. Keep all gear and bags in sealed bins in the vehicle — never loose on the seats.
  4. Never sit on the customer's furniture or set bags on infested floors/beds.

Before you go home:

  1. Change out of work clothes; seal them in a bag.
  2. Wash on the hottest setting and dry on high heat for 30+ minutes as soon as possible.
  3. Inspect bags, shoes, and any tools before they enter your home. When in doubt, steam them.
2 Unloading the Optima XD
  1. Park on level ground, set the parking brake, and chock the wheels if on a trailer.
  2. Inspect for diesel leaks, loose fittings, or damage before moving it.
  3. Use the ramp and a team lift — never muscle it down alone.
  4. Keep the unit upright and stable; secure the cart/wheels once down.
  5. Confirm fuel and water levels before transporting it to the work area.
Diesel unit — no smoking or open flame near the machine or fuel. Clean up any fuel spills immediately.
3 Setting Up the Machine
  1. Place on a level, stable surface in a well-ventilated area — the diesel burner produces exhaust.
  2. Fill the water tank with clean water per the manual; check the diesel fuel level.
  3. Connect the hose and wand; confirm all fittings are tight and undamaged.
  4. Power on and let the unit reach operating temperature/pressure (watch the LCD).
  5. Test steam onto a rag first to clear condensation and confirm dry steam before treating.
Never run the diesel burner in an enclosed space without ventilation — carbon monoxide risk. Keep the machine outside or in open air and run the hose in.
4 Disinfecting Beds, Mattresses & Couches

Technique: hold the wand ~1–2 inches off the surface and move slowly (about 1 inch per second) so the heat dwells long enough to kill bugs and eggs. Overlap each pass.

Beds & mattresses:

  1. Strip all bedding and bag it on the spot for hot laundering.
  2. Steam mattress seams, tufts, edges, and both sides; then the box spring, frame, headboard, and slats.
  3. Treat baseboards and cracks near the bed.

Couches & upholstery:

  1. Remove cushions; steam both sides, all seams, zippers, and piping.
  2. Steam the cushion cavity, frame crevices, skirt, and underside.
Move slowly for the kill — speed is the enemy. Don't oversaturate; let items dry. Re-inspect and schedule a follow-up visit.
5 Cleaning on Boats (Marine Procedure)
Confined cabins + diesel exhaust = carbon monoxide danger. Keep the machine in open air on the dock/deck and run the steam hose into the cabin. Ensure cross-ventilation.
  1. Secure the machine against boat/dock movement; mind the gap between dock and vessel.
  2. Watch electrical safety around water; keep cords and connections dry.
  3. Protect electronics and sensitive upholstery from excess moisture — use less steam, dry as you go.
  4. Be aware of confined-space and slip hazards; work with a partner.
  5. Follow the same slow-pass steaming technique for berths, cushions, and seams.
6 Shutting Down the Machine
  1. Follow the manual's shutdown sequence — turn off the burner first, then the pump.
  2. Safely relieve system pressure per the manual before disconnecting anything.
  3. Let the wand, hose, and attachments cool before handling.
  4. Power down via the LCD/controls; switch off the unit.
Steam and metal parts stay dangerously hot after shutdown. Never disconnect a hose under pressure.
7 Putting the Machine Away & Storage
  1. Drain/empty water per the manual to prevent scale buildup (if instructed for your model).
  2. Coil the hose, secure the wand and attachments.
  3. Wipe the unit down and inspect for hitchhiking bed bugs before loading it into the vehicle or storage.
  4. Re-secure the unit for transport; store in a dry place out of the weather.
  5. Log fuel/water levels and any maintenance needs for the next job.
The machine itself can carry bugs between jobs — a quick external steam-down and inspection protects your next customer and your vehicle.
