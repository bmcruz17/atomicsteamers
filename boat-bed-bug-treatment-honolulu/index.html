Why Steam On the Water

The right way to treat a vessel

On a boat, chemicals are a problem — runoff into the harbor, fumes in tight cabins, and risk around wiring and electronics. Steam is just heat and water: it kills bed bugs and their eggs on contact and reaches deep into bunk seams and mattresses, with nothing left behind.

  • No chemical runoff — nothing washes into the ocean.
  • Safe around electronics — controlled, targeted dry steam.
  • No fumes — crew can get back aboard sooner.
  • Reaches tight spaces — bunks, seams, lockers, and cabins.

Vessel Treatment Questions

Is steam safe on a boat with electronics?

Yes. We use controlled, targeted dry steam and work carefully around wiring, panels, and sensitive gear — no soaking, no chemicals.

Does anything run off into the water?

No. Steam is only heat and water — there's no pesticide or chemical runoff into the harbor or ocean.

Can you treat between trips or charters?

Yes — we work for fast turnaround so your vessel is back in service quickly, with no airing-out period.

Do you offer recurring prevention for vessels?

Yes. We set up preventative steam schedules around your trips to keep crew quarters bed-bug-free, with documentation on request.
